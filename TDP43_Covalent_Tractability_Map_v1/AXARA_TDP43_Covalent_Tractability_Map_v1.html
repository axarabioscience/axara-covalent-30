Literature note (May 2026): Published literature (Pirie et al. 2021, PNAS) identifies CYS173 as a redox-active covalent site forming a vicinal disulfide pair with CYS175 under S-nitrosylation/oxidative stress conditions. In the folded RRM1 structure (PDB 4BS2 used here), C173 is buried (~12.7 Å from the nearest pocket centroid) and excluded by the engine's accessibility filter. The engine's top recommendations (LYS140, TYR214) are structurally accessible probe sites for native folded TDP-43. For redox-stressed or partially-unfolded TDP-43 (relevant to ALS aggregation pathology), CYS173 may be the more biologically meaningful target — consider both depending on your experimental context.
