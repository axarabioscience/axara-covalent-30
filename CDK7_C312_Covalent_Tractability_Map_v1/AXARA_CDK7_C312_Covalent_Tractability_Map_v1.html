CDK7 covalent probe analysis is in active development. Both the free Tractability Map and the full paid Probe Design Map for CDK7 are scheduled for release once an engine refactor (multi-chain PDB support) is complete. Other Covalent 30 targets are available now — see axara.bio/data-products.
Why a status page
CDK7 occupies a distinctive structural niche. The covalent C312 residue sits in a C-terminal extension that traverses the ATP cleft and is only resolved in heterotrimeric CDK7/cyclin H/MAT1 co-crystal structures (e.g. PDB 6XD3, where CDK7 is chain I). Single-chain monomeric CDK7 structures available in the PDB (e.g. 1UA2) truncate at residue ~311 and do not capture C312. The AXARA engine currently expects single-chain PDBs with chain A; a refactor is in progress to support the heterotrimer-only structure required for CDK7.
Scientific context
THZ1 (Kwiatkowski et al. 2014, Nature 511:616) established C312 as a remote covalent target outside the canonical CDK7 kinase domain, providing an unprecedented selectivity mechanism. Selective second-generation inhibitors including YKL-5-124 (Olson et al. 2019, Cell Chem Biol) and clinical candidates (SY-5609, ICEC0942) build on this mechanism. T-cell acute lymphoblastic leukaemia (T-ALL) and triple-negative breast cancer show exceptional sensitivity to CDK7 covalent inhibition.
